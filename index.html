Restaurant Reviewer Dies After Confrontation at Local Filipino Restaurant

Police investigate after Derek Winter collapses at Tita Rosie’s Kitchen, a struggling family restaurant he had repeatedly criticized online.

By Andy Liao, Staff Reporter

Published: May 6, 2026

6 min read

What Happened

  • Derek Winter, a local newspaper food critic and blogger known for harsh reviews, died after collapsing at Tita Rosie’s Kitchen.
  • The incident took place inside the family-run Filipino restaurant operated by Rosie “Tita Rosie” Macapagal, Lola Flor, and Lila Macapagal.
  • Winter arrived with Edwin Long, the shopping plaza landlord and Winter’s stepfather, who had come to inspect the restaurant because it was behind on rent.
  • Witnesses said Winter insulted the restaurant, criticized Filipino food, and argued with Lila shortly before becoming visibly ill.
  • Several regulars, including older Filipino women known locally as the “Calendar Crew,” watched as Winter convulsed and collapsed face-first into a dessert bowl.

Police Investigation

Police have opened an investigation into Winter’s death after he was taken from the restaurant alive but unconscious and later died at the hospital. Officials have not publicly released a final cause, though the case is being treated as suspicious after Long accused the restaurant of killing his stepson.

Lila Macapagal, 25, has drawn police attention because she works at the restaurant, argued with Winter before his collapse, and shares a complicated history with him as a former boyfriend. Police have not announced a formal conclusion, and community members remain divided over how much suspicion should fall on her.

Witnesses also noted that Winter appeared unwell during the meal. Several people described him as sweaty, pale, shaky, thirsty, and erratic; Long reportedly asked him whether he had taken his insulin before the collapse.

Longstanding Tension

Winter’s reviews had long been a source of tension between him and the Macapagal family. His posts repeatedly framed the restaurant’s popularity as a matter of neighborhood loyalty rather than merit, a criticism that many regulars saw as insulting to both the food and the family behind it.

The conflict was also personal. Winter was Lila’s ex-boyfriend and first love, and his public criticism landed at a vulnerable time: Lila had recently returned from Chicago after a broken engagement and a failed café dream, hoping to help save the family restaurant.

For a small restaurant under financial pressure, especially one built around family recipes and regular customers, a negative review can carry more weight than a single disappointed meal. In Shady Palms, Winter’s words moved quickly from the newspaper and review sites into everyday conversation, turning a local business dispute into a community argument.

Restaurant History and Culture

Tita Rosie’s Kitchen is a family-run Filipino restaurant where food, memory, and hospitality are closely connected. The meal served to Winter’s table included adobo, longganisa, pandesal, suman, ginataang bilo-bilo, ube crinkle cookies, ube halaya, and vanilla ice cream.

Investigators are also reviewing allergy and ingredient details, including Edwin Long’s coconut allergy and the coconut-based ginataang bilo-bilo served during the meal.

Community Reaction

Community reaction has been divided between loyalty to the Macapagal family and concern over the suspicious timing of Winter’s collapse. Police are expected to release more information after witness interviews, hospital reports, and food evidence are reviewed.
